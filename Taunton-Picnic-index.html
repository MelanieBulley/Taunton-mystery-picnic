This is a self-guided, roughly 1-mile mystery walk around Taunton town centre. At each stop you’ll:

  • Scan a QR code (or use the navigation buttons).

1. Start at Stop 1 – Castle Green.
2. At each stop, scan its QR code to jump straight to that section (for example #stop2, #stop3, etc.).
3. Follow the walking directions to the next stop. The whole loop stays in the city centre and ends in Vivary Park for your picnic.

History · Taunton Castle & Castle Green

Taunton Castle’s origins reach back to the 12th century. Over the centuries it has seen sieges, political drama, and even played a role in the Monmouth Rebellion of 1685, when James Scott attempted to overthrow King James II.

Castle Green in front of you, once part of the castle precinct, is now a peaceful open space. Today it becomes your “base camp” for this mystery walk, often hosting markets with local food, crafts, and produce – perfect for gathering the first pieces of your picnic.

Picnic idea: Pick up fresh bread, pastries or local treats from market stalls when they’re running.

History · St James Street & Trade

St James Street grew around a medieval priory and later became a route linking the town to routes heading west and to the river. As Taunton developed, this area blended religious, residential and commercial uses.

Today it’s quieter than the main high street but still carries the echo of centuries of trade – a fitting place to sample rich, modern flavours in a historic setting.

Picnic idea: Pick up olives, cured meats, dips or salads that travel well. These will pair nicely with the bread you may have collected earlier.

History · Taunton Markets & Dairy

Somerset has a long history of dairy farming. Markets in Taunton would once have been busy with traders selling butter, milk, and of course cheese – a staple food that kept well before refrigeration.

As you choose your picnic cheese, you’re taking part in a pattern of trade that has shaped this region for hundreds of years.

Picnic idea: Choose a single small wedge of local cheese plus some crackers. You now have bread + deli + cheese.

The Atbash cipher works by “flipping” the alphabet: A↔Z, B↔Y, C↔X, and so on.

Encoded phrase:

How to solve:

History · Independent Shops & Growth

As Taunton expanded in the 18th and 19th centuries, many side streets and lanes filled with small, independent businesses – grocers, apothecaries, cloth merchants and more.

Shops like Ganesha continue that tradition: specialist, personal, and often focused on quality goods. They’re the modern descendants of historic market stalls and small family-run stores.

Picnic idea: Pick up fruit, nuts, or a soft drink here to balance out breads and cheeses.

History · Tea Rooms in Taunton

Tea rooms became popular in Britain in the 19th century, especially from the Victorian era onward. They offered a more respectable space for socialising than pubs, particularly for women.

Modern independent tea & coffee houses continue that role: somewhere between home and work where conversations, plans and – in your case – clues can unfold.

Picnic idea: Buy biscuits, brownies or a slice of cake that travels well, or even some loose-leaf tea to brew later at home.

History · Medieval Plots & Narrow Streets

Lanes like Bath Place often owe their shape to medieval land plots – long and narrow strips of land running back from the main road. Buildings were squeezed into these shapes, creating the charming, slightly crooked streets you see today.

Bath Place is particularly known for its mix of independent boutiques, cafes and specialist shops, making it a little micro-world within Taunton’s centre.

Picnic idea: Look for chocolates, sweets or a small special treat to finish off your picnic menu.

History · Bakers of the Town

Bakers have been essential to town life for centuries. In many places, bread was once regulated to ensure fair prices and good quality, because it was so central to everyday diets.

A modern artisan bakery carries echoes of that history – with skilled bakers, early starts, and fresh loaves appearing before most people are awake.

Picnic idea: Choose a savoury pastry, pasty or sweet bun as your “grand finale” picnic item.

History · From Vivary to Park

The name “Vivary” comes from the medieval term for a place where fish were kept – a reminder that this land once served as a resource for local people.

Over time, it has become one of Taunton’s best-loved public parks: lawns, ornamental gardens, and water features provide a breathing space right beside the busy town centre. It’s a fitting end point for a walk that’s mixed food, history and puzzles.

Picnic moment: Lay everything out – bread, deli, cheese, fruit, sweets, pastries – and enjoy the view. You’ve walked a mile, solved ciphers, and followed in the footsteps of centuries of Taunton life.
